How to Pick Great Stocks

Finding the best stocks to invest in can be very difficult at times, considering the hundreds of thousands of companies to choose from. However, when focusing on key financial metrics and fundamentals, it is easy to filter out the good stocks from the bad. The four main categories to look at when analyzing a stock are profitability, valuation, growth, and performance. Investing in companies that are strong in all four categories is the key to long term success. Companies that are underwhelming in any category pose a risk that should be accounted for, however, it could still turn out to be a good investment.
Key Profitability Metrics: - Net Margin - Operating Margin - Return on Equity Key Valuation Metrics: - Price to Earnings Ratio - Price to Sales Ratio - Price to Earnings Growth Ratio Key Growth Metrics: - Revenue Growth Y/Y - EPS Growth Y/Y - Working Capital Growth Y/Y Key Performance Metrics: - 1 Year Change (%) - 3 Month Change (%) - 6 Month Change (%)
Finding stocks that are in good standings for all four categories can be very difficult, it would be it much easier to look at one numerical rating to judge whether a stock is good to invest in or not.
